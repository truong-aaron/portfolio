Hyundai

exploring micro-mobility & Robotxi interactions

OVERVIEW



Project Tags: UX research, Micro-mobility, Robotaxis


Duration: Oct 2018 - Mar 2019



In collaboration with Hyundai, I was part of a student research group led by Design Lab faculty to explore aspects of mobility & autonomous vehicle design for Hyundai:


  • Colleen Emmenegger (Automation Director of the Design Lab),
  • Don Norman (Director of the Design Lab),
  • Stephanie Sherman (Spec. Design Graduate Student),
  • Ash Eliza Smith (Director of the CAT Program)




This project is under NDA. While I won’t get into specifics of prototypes, research results, etc., you can ask me more about my role, experience, & takeaways!

MY ROLE



My involvement for this internship consisted primarily of:


  • conducting research on micro-mobility spectrum
    • ethnographic study on how users commute
    • identify opportunities in that space for Hyundai

  • observing passenger interaction with mobility/travel
    • how do users interact with different modalities, ie. rideshares, public transit, etc.
    • how do users interact with the space & other commuters in different forms of transportation

  • collecting data concerning passengers’ first/last mile experiences
    • creation of research artifacts, such as journey maps
    • propose and prototype design opportunities for Hyundai



Example of early research artifacts developed:
journey map of an interviewee’s daily commute.



I also assisted the Robotaxi group in their research and design of an internal AV space and how users would interact within this environment and interfaces.



HIGHLIGHTS




Early look into the mobility spectrum mapping process


One of the major contributions that I can share was the mapping and analysis of the Mobility Spectrum with my partner Danielle Zusman.

We analyzed the multiple current forms of mobility & use cases, recognizing a need for AV/micro-mobility development in a specific area that Hyundai could focus their prototyping on.

Our findings and research artifacts helped guide and narrow down the avenues the team needed to explore to inform the construction of the Robotaxi prototype.
