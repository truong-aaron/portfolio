Exploring Mobility with Hyundai


OVERVIEW

Team: Listed Below




Project Tag: UX research, Micro-mobility, Robotaxis




Duration: Oct 2018 - Mar 2019

In collaboration with Hyundai, a team of student researchers were led by faculty (listed below) from the Design Lab to explore aspects of mobility + autonomous vehicle design for Hyundai:


  • Colleen Emmenegger (Automation Director of the Design Lab),
  • Don Norman (Director of the Design Lab), 
  • Stephanie Sherman (Spec. Design Graduate Student), 
  • & Ash Eliza Smith (Director of the CAT Program)

This project is under NDA. While I can’t get into specifics of prototypes, research results, etc., you can contact me for more about my role, experience, and what I learned!

MY ROLE

My involvement for this internship consisted of:


  • conducting research on micro-mobility spectrum

  • observing mobility/travel in action

  • collecting data concerning passengers’ first/last mile experiences


Additionally, I assisted Robotaxi group in their research and design of an internal AV space and how users would interact within this environment and interfaces.




An example of some of the research artifacts created:
journey map of an interviewee’s daily commute.

HIGHLIGHTS


Early look into the mobility spectrum mapping process



One of the major contributions that I can share was the mapping and analysis of the Mobility Spectrum with my partner Danielle Zusman.   

We analyzed the multiple current forms of mobility their use cases, helping the team realize a need of AV/micro-mobility development in a specific area.   

This research artifact and our findings helped guide and narrow down the avenues the team needed to explore and inform the Robotaxi team’s prototype construction.

CONCLUSION



Over the course of this internship, I gained valuable experience with conducting ethnographic studies and utilizing research to guide product development.  

While I cannot discuss in depth the findings and designs from my internship, I can elaborate more about my role and what I learned from this amazing experience.

Please feel free to reach out to me for more information regarding my involvement!
