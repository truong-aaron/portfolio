Dexcom + Diabetes Design Initiative


Team: Junhee Chung, Staci Lin, & Fiona Wong


Project Tag: Product Design, UX/UI Design, Research


Duration: June 2020 - Sept 2020

Introduction



For the better part of the last year, I have had the wonderful opportunity to be part of the Design Lab’s Diabetes Design Initiative.

  During this time, DDI was working in collaboration with local San Diego company, Dexcom. I, myself, participated in three different design sprints, each with unique objectives and teams. I primarily worked on research, testing, and developing speculative prototypes for the Dexcom Follow app.

To keep this portfolio entry from being too long, I will only be discussing my experience and insights from the latest sprint that took place from June 2020 to September 2020.



*Note* I will not disclose major research findings, prototypes, wire-frames, etc. in accordance with my NDA with Dexcom. I can, however, discuss more about my role and experience, so feel free to ask me for more!

Context


Dexcom: a San Diego based company that designs, develops, and produces CGMs for PWDs (persons with diabetes).

CGM: stands for continuous glucose monitoring, a device that provides real-time updates of a PWD’s blood glucose levels to your smart devices.

Dexcom Follow app: a companion app where Followers of a person with diabetes can also view their sharer’s real-time CGM updates

Objective


For my final design sprint with Dexcom and DDI, my team was tasked with the redesign and improvement of the Follow app.

  Our objective was to design new functionality to implement and update features in the Follow app (along with a widget) to address the wide spectrum of user needs.

Design Process


User Research


For our research phase, our team conducted:  

Comparative analysis of competitors’ CGM apps, Affinity mapping of Follow app reviews, & Interviews with 20 users of the Follow app  

The findings were affinity mapped, facilitating our team’s realization of these design opportunities:  

1. Increase accessibility to more CGM data
2. Greater customization for app and notifications
3. Recall and strategically utilize previous CGM data

User Testing


After developing our design opportunities into prototypes, we conducted usability testing with Follow app users. This was conducted over Zoom and prototype sharing on Figma.   





Testing with users as we continued iterating was not only valuable from a usability standpoint, but also help us validate our design choices. Speaking and asking actual users questions and receiving their honest feedback guided our team and assured us that our approach was actually wanted and approved by Follow users.

Prototype Iterations


Over the course of the sprint, Follow app redesign and widget were constantly changing as we received user and mentor feedback.  

During this time period, we met weekly with our Dexcom mentors from the Follow Design team to share our progress, walk through concepts, and update stakeholders.

Deliverable


At the end of the sprint, our team presented to the Dexcom stakeholders our:  

Tested designs for a new Follow app and iOS widget + extensive user research findings.


We received approval and received great feedback from the Dexcom stakeholders, which you can read more about here & here!

Conclusion


After 7 months and 3 different design sprints with DDI and the Dexcom team I have learned a great deal about working with industry professionals, remote research/testing, and presenting my work to actual stakeholders.

I have grown a huge appreciation for the continuous updates of applications, regardless of scale, as there is so much work behind the scenes to ensure everything is as users expect.

Thank-you once again to my fellow DDI teammates, mentors, and the Dexcom Follow team for such an educating experience and opportunity.

While I was unable to disclose much about my work, I am able to talk more about what I have learned, experienced, and developed from this internship. So, feel free to ask me more questions!
