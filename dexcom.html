Dexcom

prototyping an updated Dexcom Follow

OVERVIEW



Team: Junhee Chung, Staci Lin, & Fiona Wong



Project Tag: Product Design, UX/UI Design, Research



Duration: June 2020 - Sept 2020



Over the course of three unique design sprints, as part of the Design Lab’s Diabetes Design Initiative, I worked with Dexcom on the Dexcom Follow app as a student Product Designer.

My involvement included conducting research & testing, proposing redesigns, and developing prototypes for the Dexcom Follow app.

In this portfolio entry, I will primarily discuss my experience from the most recent design sprint.


This project is under NDA. While I won’t get into specifics of prototypes, research results, etc., you can ask me more about my role, experience, & takeaways!

CONTEXT



Dexcom: company that designs, develops, and produces CGMs for PWDs (persons with diabetes) that can connect to mobile devices.


CGM: aka “continuous glucose monitor”, a device that provides real-time updates of a PWD’s blood glucose levels to companion apps.


Dexcom Follow: a companion app where friends & relatives of a person with diabetes can view their sharer’s real-time CGM updates.


OVERVIEW






For the latest design sprint, my team was tasked with prototyping redesigns and improvements for the Follow app to better suit user needs.

We were given a “How might we” statement to guide our team as we began conducting preliminary research with users to indentify what a new version of Follow should contain.

Given the broadness of our prompt, needfinding was to be conducted to narrow down what should be focused on within the sprint’s scope.

NEEDFINDING



As part of our needfinding, our team conducted the following:

  • Comparative analysis of competitors’ CGM apps
    • “What functionality does Follow have/doesn’t have compared to others?”
    • “What are the painpoints users currently face?”

  • Affinity mapping of Follow app reviews
    • Categorizing reviews based on similar sentiments
    • Identifying cause of negative reviews, ”Is it something design can solve or is it technical?”

  • Interviews with 20 users of the Follow app
    • Walk us through their typical flow
    • Taking note of recurring points/needs users bring up


Affinity mapped insights from user interviews



The results were then compiled into affinity maps to identify potential opportunity points to prioritize for this sprint.

We pinpointed three main design opportunities for the new version of Follow that would address the most common needs and concerns that users experienced.





Stakeholders in Dexcom and our project managers approved of our approach, and we began conceptualizing & prototyping for these core design goals.

PROTOTYPING



Since this was during the height of the pandemic, our team had to conduct everything remotely, including prototyping. As such, our primary tools were Figma, Zoom, and digital sketching to maximize collaboration and input.








I can’t disclose further specifics about our prototypes, but you can ask me more about how we as a team addressed the opportunity points and our process!

USER TESTING



Since we were remote at the time, we conducted prototype testing with Follow users over Zoom using Figma’s prototyping feature. 






Without getting into specifics, we had frequent testing sessions as we iterated, which were invaluable! It helped validate and inform further design modifications as we continued developing the prototypes.


By testing with current users of Follow app, we got a better sense of how important it was in their daily lives. As such, their insightful painpoints and needs guided further development changes.

DELIVERABLE



At the end of the sprint, our team presented to the Dexcom stakeholders tested, high fidelity designs for a new Follow app and iOS widget that addressed the design opportunities our team pinpointed from user research. 






The proposals and prototypes were further divided into different levels of priority for implementation based on their importance to users, validated by our research and test findings.


Our concepts well received and given great feedback from the Dexcom design team (which you can read more about here.) Our test users were also estatic that their needs, for such an essential tool in their lives, were finally being addressed!

REFLECTION



After three wonderful design sprints with DDI & Dexcom team, I have learned a great deal about working with industry professionals, remote research/testing, and presenting my work to stakeholders.
