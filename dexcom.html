OVERVIEW

Team: Junhee Chung, Staci Lin, & Fiona Wong




Project Tag: Product Design, UX/UI Design, Research




Duration: June 2020 - Sept 2020




I had the wonderful opportunity to be part of the Design Lab’s Diabetes Design Initiative, working in collaboration with local San Diego bio-tech company: Dexcom.

  I participated in three design sprints (10 weeks each), researching, testing, and developing prototypes for the Dexcom Follow app.

In this portfolio entry, I will primarily discuss my experience from the most recent design sprint.



I can’t discuss major findings, prototypes, wire-frames, etc. in accordance with my NDA. I can, however, talk more about my role and experiences, so feel free to ask!

CONTEXT

Dexcom: a San Diego based company that designs, develops, and
produces CGMs for PWDs (persons with diabetes).


CGM: stands for continuous glucose monitor, a device that provides
real-time updates of a PWD’s blood glucose levels to their smart devices.


Dexcom Follow: a companion app where Followers of a person with
diabetes can view their sharer’s real-time CGM updates.


OBJECTIVE



For the latest design sprint, my team was tasked with an overall redesign and improvement of the Follow app.   

Our objective was to update existing features and prototype new functionality to implement in the Follow app, along with a widget,to address a wide spectrum of user needs.

DESIGN PROCESS

User Research




Affinity mapped insights from user interviews


For our needfinding + ressearch, our team conducted:

  • Comparative analysis of competitors’ CGM apps
  • Affinity mapping of Follow app reviews
  • Interviews with 20 users of the Follow app

User Testing




Iterating on the design opportunities, we began preliminary prototype testing with current Follow app users.  

These were conducted over Zoom through prototype sharing on Figma.





While I can’t get into specifics about our testing process, I can say that it was invaluable to test as we iterated, helping validate design choices early on and narrowing down previously overlooked user needs.

Prototype Iterations




Over the course of the sprint, the Follow app redesign and widget were constantly changing as we received user and mentor feedback.  

We met weekly with our mentors from the Follow design team to share our progress and walk through our prototypes.



Deliverable







At the end of the sprint, our team presented to the Dexcom stakeholders tested, high fidelity designs for a new Follow app and iOS widget that addressed the design opportunities our team pinpointed from user research. 

Our concepts were well received and were given great feedback from the Dexcom stakeholders, which you can read more about here!

REFLECTION

After three different design sprints with DDI and the Dexcom team, I have learned a great deal about working with industry professionals, remote research/testing, and presenting my work to stakeholders.

Thank-you once again to my fellow DDI teammates, mentors, and the Dexcom Follow team for such an educating experience and opportunity.
